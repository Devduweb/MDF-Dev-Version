Installation

This mod is pre-installed. Just download, unpack, thats it. When you are reading this, you already installed the mod, congratulation. Please do not copy it over vanilla DF or older mod versions. Just unpack it into a new folder.

To alter the mod, you can run the Masterwork Dwarf Fortress.exe. You will need to do this to set Succubus as a playable race. Just open it up, click on 'Races' and you will see a large list of civilizations. Currently Dwarves, Orcs, Kobolds, Gnomes, Warlocks and Succubi are potentially playable. Tick the box under 'Fort Mode' and all is done.

If you just want to play one race, it is recommended to turn the other races non-playable. It will make embarking easier, since you dont have to chose your race ingame.

How to do a bug report

When you report bugs, please state the mod version and the tileset you use. If you run the mod on Mac or Linux, please state this as well. I need this kind of info, otherwise I cant search in the right places.

If your bug is related to the civilization itself (problem with workshop, caravans, etc.), please report it to the race's dedicated thread.

Getting Started

The succubi are a race of lesser demons who like settling away from the abyss. They are intelligent, strong, and extremely agile. On the other hand, hey are not very good archers, they take breaks often and are quick to anger.

Their metalurgy is also lacking when it come to protection. They cannot make steel and their armor does not protect well. This is why they must rely on dodging and blocking. Something they have little trouble to achieve with enough training.

The race has an heavy gender inbalance or 9 females to 1 male. This leads to your fort getting few children to take care of. However, you should be careful to protect married couples and their children as a tragedy can lead to simultaneous tantrums.

Gameplay wise, the civilization is between dwarves and warlocks. You will be able to upgrade your fighter with powerful spells, and use creature specialized in military or civilian uses. Production and management however is similar to a standard race.

The game is, as a whole, easier while playing as the succubi. Several pitfalls can be avoided and the race's feature are additive, enabling you to learn the feature at your own pace.

The Embark

The succubi use a mix of surface and underground features. They enjoy expansive palace where dark stone and gem windows meet decorated furniture.  Each fort in their kingdom must be treated as a work of art.

But the finely decorated bedrooms hide a darker secret. In the underground, dark temples maintain a link between the surface and the horrors below, enabling the succubi to summon powerful monsters while the witches sacrify the souls of their enemies for their own leisure, or their enemies pain.

If possible, succubi prefer to embark on evil biomes. They particulary enjoy drinking wine made from evil plants and their cattle does not suffer there. Beward of the undead who will never understand desires the way lust demons do.

The Embark Preparation

You will be able to live with a standard inventory. However you have to keep in mind that sieges will start very early. So it is a good idea to prepare one of your original succubi as a trained fighter.


Skills:

- Same as dwarves for 6 succubi : 2 miners, a lumberjack, a carpenter, a mason, a farmer
- Idealy one combat capable succubus to appoint as lady of pain.
- Do not separate organizer and bookkeeper. The keeper of secrets position fill both roles.

Items:

- Standard items a regular fort uses. What worked for dwarves will for for the succubi.
- Your succubi are not alcohol dependant so drinks are not as vital if there is water.
- You can buy surface plants at the embark screen.
- A weapon and an helmet to equip your lady of pain. Bronze is the best budget metal.

Pets:

- Tentacle monsters are expensive but will provides an oil substitute, sets contaminant traps and are good fighters.
- Orthus are similar to dogs but stronger, they can serve as bodyguards.
- Nahashes or rat things will hunt vermins around.
- You will start with 2 cauchemars. Those can follow in simple fights or provide your with early milk.

First things on the map

First appoit your lady of pain and create a squad for her. Get her to train quickly and give her 2 sparring mates after the first migration wave. Be careful of her happines however, digging a room for her and making early furniture with your carpenter is a good idea.

Otherwise the standard procedure to securing your supplies and making the fort sustainable and defended apply. You must keep in mind that 'good' guys are on the way so a drawbridge and basic defences are a priority.

Afterwards you can slaughter your starting cauchemars. Turn their skulls into totems and you will be able to build a summoning circle and start bringing extra beasts. Their souls can be used at the temple of sin to offer a couple of extra moves to your warriors.

Advancing

Build a jeweler workshop and start cutting gems. Those will let you conserve souls at the Soul Well. Large gems also allow you to summon fire imps.

The temple of fire can add fire immunity to your warriors, combining that with fire imps, torrid kiss or other fire interactions will give you an edge. 

You can also make a worker fire immune and start digging space for a magma reservoir with a magma well designed to pour into it. Use your fire immune succubi to summon magma. Buliding your furnaces on top of those will unlock most of the industry.

As an alternative to immigrants, making a large quantity of cage traps will allow you to turn prisoners into more succubi. Build their cage near a den of iniquity and, afterwards, free them using a lever. Be careful that their mount will remain hostile no matter what.

You can seek better armor materials, summoned basilisks can be sheared for horns. Turning their horns into bars in the smelter will yield a metal as strong as steel but much lighter. Basiliskine is great for armor making.
